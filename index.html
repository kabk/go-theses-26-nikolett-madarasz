Atmosphere

The keys are ringing as they hit each other, while trying to open the front door. As I step inside, the busy noise from the street stays behind me, and silence is welcoming me. I can hear my soft steps on the carpeted stairs, walking up after a long day of work. I turn on the kettle and walk into the living room, opening the curtains. The sunlight shines through the embroidered curtains my mother gave me, creating a beautiful pattern on the dining table and the wall. The table, with four chairs, standing stands in front of the window, some of the chairs have stuff hanging on them, as stuff no one uses it, but signifiesremembering that once we all sat here together. There is the embroidered table cover, with flowers in the middle, the open book left there with her reading glasses, and the leftovers of the breakfast. The kettle is loud in the background, and the clock is ticking. Through the open window the birds chirping can be heard inside. There is an empty cigarette box, some opened cookies, and empty plates. The mug with the freshly poured tea is steaming, and the curtains are softly moveing by the window. The hair brush is placed down carefully in the corner of the table, alongside a small mirror. Someone is hanging out their clothes on the dryer, the soft slams of the clothes in the air resonate and fill up the silence. The red persian carpet under the table is soft and delicate, making it comfortable to sit down and put your feet down. The picture frames are a bit grey even though they represent happy memories, and the plants on the shelves need some watering. When the night comes and the sun sets down, the window closes, but still you can hear the light chirping of grasshoppers in the background, and their singing holds up the space, creating a comfort in this silence. The curtains move back covering the window, and the soft light of the standing lamp turns off. Nothing was touched on the table, it feels too sacred to move them. The mug, the plate, the empty box with the lighter, the same page on the opened book, and the reading glasses, cheating a space that only exists here and within you.
